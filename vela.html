Weather App: Vela App for water-sport enthusiast

As a part of my Student Project, my challenge was to identify a problem in the current weather Apps, and solve the problem using a user-centered design.Free Weather apps currently in the market provide you with very generic information which leaves the water sports enthusiasts asking for more. Furthermore the forecast is not for the exact spot you were looking for but limits to the closest city nearby.  Another problem is the amount of information on a screen that made it difficult to read, made even more difficult when you view it on the small mobile phone screen with the sun in your eyes. My solution was to create an easy to read personalised weather App, which gives all the relevant weather detail at a single glance, so you can keep on enjoying the sport you love with no stress about weather.

Step by Step Design Solution

Understanding my Product and Market

While talking to my friends, family and neighbours, I discovered that they would love to have a personalised app with a simple interface and the weather forecast in an easy to read format.  To create the design solution, I started with Competitor Analysis and Market surveys and interviews to help me understand the market- the available products and services.  Below you see the comparison between Windy and PredictWind, the current market leaders in the weather App market.

Developing my User Persona

Based on the Market Research and Interviews, I discovered what people like, the problems and frustrations they have with the available weather apps. Using all that information I developed User Personas to keep me on track on what is important while I create the solution for them.  ​​Below you see one of the Persona developed for Vela- Mark.  ​​​​​​​

Sample of the Prototype

Then I created User Experience for my Personas to gave me an understanding in the relationship between the product and the users. Based on that I designed the Wireframes. ​​​​​​​This design was then tested in-person with different users to identify the problem areas. I used this feedback to Iterate and improve the product before starting with my Prototype designing.  After testing the app with the users, I realised that hourly forecast and back button was an important feature which was missing in my early sketches. I also understood the importance of an interesting and informative onboarding screen. After all the Testing and the Feedbacks the Design evolved.
